## Sheenah Work
<!-- Active focus areas from therapy with Sheenah Hankin (Complete Confidence framework).
     These are live, recurring themes — not resolved. Surface them during reflection. -->

**Disappointing people on purpose.**
Sheenah's practice: deliberately disappoint someone every day as exposure therapy for criticism.
The goal is to break the Pleasing Finger habit — the belief that keeping everyone happy is survival.
The muscle being built: tolerating disapproval without it collapsing into shame.

**Fear of money / "What would it take for you to go broke?"**
Sheenah's standing question to Andrew. The fear isn't poverty — it's the feeling of worthlessness that
money wards off. Until the question has a real answer, it controls behavior: staying in situations too
long, avoiding risk, conflating financial safety with self-worth.

**People pleasing.**
The Pleasing Finger: doing too much for others as a way to pre-empt criticism and manage shame.
The cost is self-abandonment — Andrew's preferences, limits, and honest reactions get suppressed.
The antidote is the same as disappointing people: practice saying no, holding a position, letting
someone be unhappy with you and surviving it.
